Artist's Statement

Rusted Impressions

Mission:

After the massive earthquake in Nepal last year, the landscape of Kathmanduhas been marked by ever increasing piles of debris. As we rebuild, these piles posesignificant challenges for us; first, we must find ways to rebuild our homes andlandmarks without simultaneously transforming the landscape into one covered inrubbish and ruin. Secondly, it is important that we find ways to memorialize both thephysical loss and the emotional toll this event caused. To draw attention to both of thesepressing needs, " am using oxidized, discarded materials and transferring their imagesto the surfaces of paper and canvas.

Process:

Using discarded metal scraps, I hope to showcase the beauty present in very ordinary objects and to explore the rich colors rust produces. Because rust is a natural pigment, it creates an unexpected variety of hues. Because no two prints ever come out exactly the same, working with rust challenges me as an artist to adapt to the medium I have choosen and to allow for parts of my artistic process to be out of my strict control
